RelAfford6D: Relational 6D Affordance Graphs for Constraint-Driven Robotic Manipulation

A training-free framework that bridges language, part-level 6D perception, and closed-loop kinematic constraint tracking for open-world manipulation.

Guodong Zhang, Qichen He, Wenyuan Xie, Shaokai Wu, Yanbiao Ji, Qiuchang Li, Bayram Bayramli, Yue Ding, Hongtao Lu

School of Computer Science, Shanghai Jiao Tong University

Abstract

RelAfford6D addresses open-world robotic manipulation by introducing a Relational 6D Affordance Graph. From a free-form instruction, the system infers a semantic topology that links a primary interacting part to its physical anchor, grounds the two parts as metric SE(3) poses, and executes manipulation as a closed-loop kinematic constraint satisfaction problem.

The resulting framework avoids task-specific training and replaces isolated contact predictions with explicit part-to-part physical relations, enabling robust manipulation of articulated and rigid objects in simulation and the real world.

Method

RelAfford6D decomposes open-world manipulation into semantic topology generation, metric visual grounding, and constraint-driven execution.

Semantic Topology Generation

A structured kinematic knowledge base and retrieval-augmented LLM reasoning infer the object, primary interacting part, anchor part, and relational action token.

Metric Visual Grounding

Vision foundation models ground semantic parts in RGB-D observations and estimate part-level 6D poses using a model-free FoundationPose pipeline.

Constraint-Driven Execution

The instantiated part relation defines revolute, prismatic, or rigid-body manifolds that the robot tracks in closed loop with online replanning.

Experiments

We evaluate RelAfford6D across articulated-object manipulation, rigid-object manipulation, robustness tests, and real-world deployment.

74% Average success on articulated-object manipulation.
100.0% Success on PickCube, StackCube, and PushCube.
5 Hz Closed-loop tracking during execution.
7 Real-world manipulation categories on RM75.
Metric visual grounding elevates part-level masks into explicit SE(3) poses for downstream control.
Closed-loop tracking detects object-state changes and re-synthesizes physically valid trajectories.
Real-world demonstrations on a Realman RM75 robot with an Intel RealSense D435 RGB-D camera.
Pipeline view of semantic perception, visual perception, and action execution modules.
